Virgin del Quinche Parade

On November 21st the local church of Bushwick, Brooklyn, St. Brigid throws an annual parade for the Virgin del Quinche. The parade is a celebration that unites Ecuadorians and Catholics in Bushwhick.

The Virgin del Quinche means the Virgin mary of Quinche. El Quinche is a city in Ecuador. The Catholic church of El Quinche holds an ancient statue of the Virgin Mary. The Virgin Mary statue is sacred in Ecuador and in Catholicism.

History of The Virgin del Quinche

The statue was originally from Oyacachi, Ecuador. It was previously named the Virgin of Oyacachi. However, in 1604 when indigenous residents of Oyacachi were not truly practicing the Catholic religion, it was relocated to the town of El Quinche and renamed the Virgin del Quiche.

The Miracles of The Virgin del Quiche

The statue of the Virgin del Quinche is known to save children from wild animals, healing the sick and comforting those who are dying. Some people also reported that her statues would go missing for a couple of days and would return with mud on her feet.
